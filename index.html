What is Organic Waste?

Organic matter comes from a living organism and when it is not living anymore, it will decay over time. Plant matter, such as fruit and vegetables can be turned into compost. Composting is important, because organic waste in landfill causes the production of methane which is really bad for the ozone layer. It's also really cool! By turning your food scraps into compost, you can help new food grow!

What are Soft Plastics?

All "scrunchable" plastic including biscuit wrappers, plastic shopping bags, garbage bags, chip packets, salad packets and bread bags - basically anything that acts as a bag around your food is soft plastic. It is also the plastic used by dry cleaners to cover your shirts, the clear plastic wrap that comes with any appliance purchase. It is everywhere! Soft plastic can be recycled and turned into other items such as heavy-duty outdoor plastic furniture and even used to build roads! It can also be chemically recycled, which turns it back into oil, that can be used for making new plastic resins for fuel and other purposes.

What are Recylable Materials?

First of all, it is important to check out what your local council accepts in your recycling bin. As a general rule, paper, cardboard, platic bottles, steel/aluminium cans and glass bottles/jars belong in your recycling bin. To be sure, always look out for the recycling logo which tells you if the item is recyclable or not.
